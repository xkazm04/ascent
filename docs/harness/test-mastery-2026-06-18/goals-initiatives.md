# Test Mastery — Goals & Initiatives

This context turns fleet scans into the leadership planning surface: goals with a
trend-derived pace verdict ("on pace / behind / reached"), an ETA, and the repos that must
move; initiatives that track the work; and a what-if simulator coupled to the goal forecast.
The pure level-band forecaster (`forecastTrajectory`) is well covered in
`src/lib/maturity/forecast.test.ts`. But the **goal-specific** projection (`projectGoal`) and
the **entire `src/lib/db/plan.ts` glue layer** — where the goal verdict, the achieved-state
write, and the progress math actually live — have **no tests at all**, and one e2e test asserts
a goal heading that the UI no longer renders. Risk lives one layer above where the tests stop.

## 1. Test `projectGoal`'s pace verdict for FAILURE, not just `forecastTrajectory`
- **Severity**: Critical
- **Category**: coverage-gap
- **File**: src/lib/maturity/forecast.ts:223 (`projectGoal`) — untested; only `forecastTrajectory`/`humanizeDays`/`forecastHeadline` are covered in src/lib/maturity/forecast.test.ts
- **Scenario**: A refactor of the verdict ladder (lines 256–260) — e.g. flipping `<=` to `<` in `Date.parse(etaDate) <= deadlineMs`, or reordering the `!hasDeadline || !fit` branch — silently turns a "behind" goal into "on pace" (or vice-versa). The ETA cap (`GOAL_ETA_CAP_DAYS = 1095`, line 211) regresses so a flat-trend goal projects a 9-year ETA as "on-pace". `requiredPerWeek` divides by `daysLeft` that just went non-positive and emits a garbage rate. Every one of these ships green: no test exercises `projectGoal`.
- **Root cause**: The forecast suite stops at the level-band forecaster. `projectGoal` is a *different* decision surface (target line vs. band boundary, deadline verdict, required-rate) and is the function `listGoals` calls for every goal — yet it has zero assertions. The "it's pure and deterministic, `nowMs` is injected" docstring is an invitation to test it that nobody took.
- **Impact**: The pace chip and the ETA are the headline a leader reads to decide whether to pour engineering effort at a maturity goal. A mislabeled "On pace" hides a slipping target; a mislabeled "Behind" triggers a needless fire drill. This is the core value proposition of the planning surface and it is unguarded.
- **Fix sketch**: Add `describe("projectGoal")` in forecast.test.ts. Inject a fixed `nowMs`. Assert the invariants: (a) `current >= target` ⇒ `pace === "reached"`, `etaDays === null`; (b) rising series + deadline that the OLS ETA lands *before* ⇒ `"on-pace"` and `etaDate <= targetDate`; (c) same series with an *earlier* deadline ⇒ `"behind"` with `requiredPerWeek > perWeek`; (d) flat/no-deadline ⇒ `"tracking"` and `etaDate === null`; (e) flat trend (`perDay === 0`) below target ⇒ `etaDays === null` (never a >1095-day ETA); (f) `requiredPerWeek === null` once the deadline is past (`daysLeft <= 0`).

## 2. Cover `listGoals`: the achieved-state write and the progress/laggard math (zero tests on `db/plan.ts`)
- **Severity**: Critical
- **Category**: coverage-gap
- **File**: src/lib/db/plan.ts:249 (`listGoals`, the `justAchieved` write at 260–306) — no plan.test.ts exists
- **Scenario**: `listGoals` does a **persisted state transition inside a read**: when `current >= target && g.status === "active"` it stamps `status="achieved"` + `achievedAt` (lines 272–275, 301–306). A regression that drops the `g.status === "active"` idempotency guard re-stamps `achievedAt` to "now" on every page load, corrupting the recorded achievement date. A change to the `pct` formula (line 283, `target > 0 ? … : 100`) or the laggard filter/sort (lines 267–271) ships a wrong progress bar and a wrong "must move" list with no test to catch it. The `target === 0` branch returning `pct: 100` is a real edge no test pins.
- **Root cause**: `src/lib/db/plan.ts` — the entire goals/initiatives data layer, ~600 lines — has **no test file**. The pure, easily-extractable pieces (`currentFor`, `repoValueFor`, the laggard reducer, the `justAchieved`/`achievedAt` decision) are buried inside the Prisma query instead of being callable, so nothing asserts them. The "best-effort persistence… re-marks on the next load" comment papers over an untested write.
- **Impact**: `achievedAt` is the audit fact for "we hit this maturity target on date X" — leadership and the executive briefing consume it. Silent re-stamping or a wrong progress percentage erodes trust in the whole standing dashboard, and the bug is invisible until someone notices a goal's celebration date keeps moving.
- **Fix sketch**: Extract the per-goal shaping into a pure helper (`shapeGoal(goalRow, snapshot, projection, nowMs)`) that returns `{view, newlyAchieved}`, then unit-test it without a DB. Assert: reaching target while `status === "active"` sets `status: "achieved"`, `achievedAt === isoOf(nowMs)`, and `newlyAchieved === true` **exactly once** (a second call with `status: "achieved"` yields `newlyAchieved === false` and preserves the original `achievedAt`); `target === 0` ⇒ `pct === 100`; laggards are sorted worst-first with `gap === target - value` and `belowCount` counts the full set even when `laggards` is truncated to 12.

## 3. Add route tests for the goals/initiatives API: authz gate, validation, and not-found
- **Severity**: High
- **Category**: coverage-gap
- **File**: src/app/api/org/goals/route.ts, src/app/api/org/goals/[id]/route.ts:14 (`gate`), src/app/api/org/initiatives/[id]/route.ts:17 — no route tests
- **Scenario**: The mutating routes are the tenant boundary for planning data. A regression that drops `requireOrgAccess(body.org)` from goals POST (line 28), or reorders the `[id]` gate so `getGoalOrgSlug` runs without the subsequent `requireOrgAccess` (lines 14–19), lets a non-member create/delete goals in another org — a cross-tenant write IDOR. Looser regressions also slip: `isGoalMetric` rejection (line 30), the `Number.isNaN(Date.parse(targetDate))` 400 (line 33), the `STATUSES` whitelist on initiatives (line 27), and the P2025 → 404 mapping (line 33 of `[id]/route.ts`) are all unguarded.
- **Root cause**: No test imports any of these handlers. Ascent already tests sibling routes (`org/import/route.test.ts`, `org/scan/route.test.ts`) by mocking `@/lib/authz` and `@/lib/db`, so the harness exists — the goals/initiatives routes just never got one.
- **Impact**: Goals and initiatives are org-scoped planning data; a silent authz drop is a confidentiality/integrity breach across tenants. The validation gaps are lower-stakes but let malformed metrics/dates/statuses reach the DB and corrupt the progress math from finding #1/#2.
- **Fix sketch**: Add `tests`-style route tests mocking `@/lib/authz` and `@/lib/db` (mirror `org/import/route.test.ts`). Assert the invariants: when `requireOrgAccess`/`gate` returns a 403 Response, the handler **returns it and never calls `createGoal`/`updateGoal`/`deleteGoal`/`createInitiative`**; POST goals with a bad `metric` ⇒ 400 and DB untouched; POST goals with `targetDate: "nonsense"` ⇒ 400; `getGoalOrgSlug → null` ⇒ 404 before any auth side effect; a Prisma `P2025` thrown by update/delete ⇒ 404 (not 500); initiatives PATCH with `status: "bogus"` ⇒ 400.

## 4. Fix the success-theater e2e: it asserts a goal heading the UI no longer renders
- **Severity**: High
- **Category**: success-theater
- **File**: e2e/org-suite/overview.spec.ts:26
- **Scenario**: The test `"goal + standing give the org direction"` asserts `getByRole("heading", { name: "Goal · reach AI-Native" })`. That hardcoded heading was **removed** — `GoalsOverview` now renders dynamic `goal.label` via `GoalCard` (see the file-header comment in src/components/org/GoalsOverview.tsx:1–3 explicitly noting it "replaced the single hardcoded 'reach AI-Native' goal"). The literal `"Goal · reach AI-Native"` exists nowhere in rendered source. So this assertion is either failing on every run (and being ignored/quarantined) or the spec isn't wired into CI — meaning the **goals overview surface has no working e2e guard at all**, while the suite reports a "goal" test as present.
- **Root cause**: The UI evolved from a fixed goal to admin-defined goals; the e2e assertion was not updated. A stale, never-passing (or never-run) test gives false confidence that the goals overview is covered.
- **Impact**: The one e2e that names "goal" doesn't exercise the real goals UI. A regression that blanks the GoalsOverview card, breaks the pace chip, or drops the "Set a goal" empty state ships unnoticed — the exact leadership-direction surface the test claims to protect.
- **Fix sketch**: Rewrite the assertion against what `GoalsOverview`/`GoalCard` actually render: with a seeded goal, assert the goal's `label`, the pace chip text (one of `Reached|On pace|Behind|Tracking`), and the `current/target` meter line; with no goals, assert the `"No goals set yet."` empty state and the `"Set a goal →"` CTA. Invariant: the test must reference a selector that exists in the current component tree, and must fail if the goals card stops rendering.

## 5. Batch-test the pure plan helpers (`metricLabel`, `isGoalMetric`, `parseRepos`, `parseTargetDate`, `dailyAvg`)
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Category**: edge-case
- **File**: src/lib/db/plan.ts:17 (`isGoalMetric`), :22 (`metricLabel`), :114 (`dailyAvg`), :167 (`parseTargetDate`), :368 (`parseRepos`)
- **Scenario**: These are small, pure, and on the hot path, yet untested. `isGoalMetric`/`VALID_METRICS` lists `D1..D9` but the model and the route regex (`/^D[1-9]$/`) must agree — a drift (e.g. a future `D10`, or `model.ts` renaming a dimension) makes `metricLabel` fall through to the raw id and `isGoalMetric` accept/reject the wrong set. `parseRepos` must survive non-array / non-string JSON (a corrupted `repos` column) without throwing inside `listInitiatives` (line 431); a regression that drops the `Array.isArray` / `typeof x === "string"` filter (lines 371) crashes the whole initiatives read. `dailyAvg` collapse-by-day mean and `parseTargetDate`'s `Number.isFinite(Date.parse())` guard are the inputs the forecaster trusts.
- **Root cause**: Pure validators/formatters that are trivially LLM-generatable were never given a batch — the fastest coverage win in the context, each asserting a real invariant rather than chasing lines.
- **Impact**: A bad `repos` JSON or a metric/dimension drift degrades quietly: a crashed initiatives panel, a mislabeled goal metric, or a malformed trend series feeding finding #1's pace math. Medium because the blast radius is one panel, not cross-tenant.
- **Fix sketch**: One `plan.test.ts` (or extract the helpers to a `plan-shared.ts`) with a table-driven batch. Invariants: `isGoalMetric` is true for exactly `{overall,adoption,rigor,D1..D9}` and false for `"D0"`,`"D10"`,`""`,`"overall "`; `metricLabel("overall"|"adoption"|"rigor")` returns the friendly strings and `metricLabel("D2")` returns the model's `DIMENSION_BY_ID.D2.name` (asserting they stay in sync), with an unknown id echoed back; `parseRepos` returns `[]` for invalid JSON, a non-array, and `[1,"a"]` ⇒ `["a"]` (never throws); `parseTargetDate(null|""|"bad")` ⇒ `null` and a valid ISO ⇒ a `Date`; `dailyAvg` collapses multiple same-day points to their rounded mean and emits points sorted ascending by date.
