# Test Mastery — Fleet Rollups & Insights

This context is the aggregation layer turning many per-repo scans into the fleet-level numbers the org dashboard, digests, and executive briefing read. The tested surface today is narrow: four pure helpers (`percentileOf`, `dueBucketFor`, `computeWindowDeltas`, `rollupTeams`) and `window.ts`. The heavyweight aggregations — contributor intelligence, PR/governance/activity signals, the period-windowed movers baseline, gap analysis, and the backlog's per-repo ROI assembly — have **zero tests**, even where their math is pure and trivially extractable. Findings are ranked by the business blast radius of a silent miscalculation that leaders see as headline truth.

## 1. Extract and test the per-period baseline selection inside getOrgMovers / getOrgRollup
- **Severity**: Critical
- **Category**: coverage-gap
- **File**: src/lib/db/org-insights.ts:80-112 (getOrgMovers windowed branch); src/lib/db/org-rollup.ts:255-292 (baseline + deltas)
- **Scenario**: A repo onboarded mid-quarter has no scan before `window.start`. `getOrgMovers` deliberately falls back to the repo's *earliest in-window* scan as the baseline (`arr.find((s) => s.scannedAt <= start) ?? arr[arr.length - 1]`), while `getOrgRollup`'s baseline cohort uses a *strict* `scannedAt: { lt: start }` and silently EXCLUDES that same repo. The two surfaces therefore disagree about which repos "moved" in the period — the movers panel shows a climb the headline tile deltas don't, or vice-versa. A boundary scan whose timestamp is exactly `start` lands in-window for the trend (`gte`) but is excluded from the baseline (`lt`) — and `getOrgMovers` would pick it as `prev`, comparing it against itself or against a later scan, fabricating or erasing movement. None of this branch logic is exercised; `computeWindowDeltas` is tested in isolation with hand-built snaps, but the *selection* of which scan becomes each cohort's baseline (the actual source of the historical "−25 fleet slip" bug) is not.
- **Root cause**: The baseline-selection rules live inline inside two large async DB functions that the suite can't reach without a database; the testable invariant (onboarded-vs-existing cohort membership, half-open boundary handling) was never factored into a pure function the way `computeWindowDeltas` was.
- **Impact**: The two flagship dashboard surfaces (period deltas and the movers/regressers panel) can present contradictory fleet movement to leadership and the weekly digest — the exact "fabricated score movement" class the cohort-matching was built to kill, now reintroducible at the *baseline-pick* layer with no test to catch it.
- **Fix sketch**: Extract a pure `pickWindowBaseline(scansDesc, start)` returning `{ prev, now } | null` with the documented onboarded-fallback rule, and a `selectBaselineCohort(priorScansDesc, start)` for the rollup side. Assert: (a) a repo with a scan exactly at `start` is treated identically by both surfaces; (b) an onboarded repo (no scan `< start`) appears in movers via earliest-in-window but is excluded from the rollup baseline cohort — and document/lock that intended asymmetry; (c) a single-in-window-scan repo yields `null` (no self-comparison). Wire both call sites through the extracted helpers.

## 2. Test contributor intelligence: bus factor, champion ranking, and AI-share weighting
- **Severity**: High
- **Category**: coverage-gap
- **File**: src/lib/db/org-contributors.ts:121-186 (getContributorInsights), :142-165 (busFactor / topShare / soloMaintainer)
- **Scenario**: `busFactor` counts contributors until cumulative commits exceed `total/2` (strict `> total/2`). With two contributors at 50/50, `acc` reaches exactly `total/2` on the first (not `>`), so it counts a bus factor of 2 — correct — but a single all-commits maintainer, or ties at the median, or a zero-commit repo (`total === 0`, guarded only by the `topShare` ternary, not the loop) are unverified. `soloMaintainer` (`length === 1 || topShare >= 80`) and `championScore = (aiShare/100)·√repoCount·log2(commits+1)` likewise have no test pinning their formula. A refactor that flips `>` to `>=`, drops the `commits + 1` (→ `log2(0)` = `-Infinity` for a 1-commit dim), or reorders the share rounding would silently mis-rank who the org's "AI champions" and single-points-of-failure are.
- **Root cause**: All of this is a pure transform over `RepoContributor` rows, yet it lives inside an un-extracted async function — unlike `rollupTeams`, which *was* extracted and tested. No equivalent `rollupContributors(rows)` seam exists.
- **Impact**: Bus-factor and champion rankings drive "who is AI-native" / "key-person risk" conclusions leaders act on (staffing, knowledge-spread). A silent off-by-one in the bus-factor loop or a sign error in `championScore` mislabels a healthy repo as solo-maintained, or crowns the wrong champion — wrong, with full dashboard authority.
- **Fix sketch**: Extract `rollupContributors(rows): ContributorInsights` (mirroring `rollupTeams`) and assert: bus factor = 1 for a single dominant maintainer and = N for an even split; `soloMaintainer` true at exactly 80% top share and false at 79%; `championScore` ordering is stable and finite for a 1-commit contributor; bots/`unknown` excluded from every aggregate; `orgAiShare` is commit-weighted (not a mean of per-person shares). Include a zero-commit repo to lock `topShare`/`busFactor` against divide-by-zero.

## 3. Test the JSON-blob signal aggregators for malformed and partial data
- **Severity**: High
- **Category**: error-branch
- **File**: src/lib/db/org-signals.ts:32-63 (prStats), :100-141 (governance), :162-183 (activity element-wise sum); src/lib/db/org-insights.ts:819-841 (discrepancies)
- **Scenario**: Four aggregators `JSON.parse` per-repo blobs persisted from earlier scans and fold them into fleet headline rates. Each has a bespoke filter: prStats keeps only `analyzed > 0`; governance drops `!readable`; activity requires a non-empty array; discrepancies skips rows lacking string `dimension`/`claim`. `getOrgActivity` then sums weekly series **right-aligned** (`offset = maxLen - s.length`) so repos with shorter histories pad on the left. A regression that mis-aligns the offset, counts a `readable:false` repo in `protectedRate`, or includes a zero-PR repo in `avgMergeRate` would skew every governance/PR/activity headline — silently, because malformed/legacy blobs are swallowed by bare `catch {}` with no test proving the *good* rows still aggregate correctly around the bad ones.
- **Root cause**: Parsing + aggregation are entangled inside async DB functions with no pure seam; the `catch {}` arms make failures invisible, so a mis-shaped blob degrades a rate silently rather than throwing. No test feeds a mix of valid/invalid blobs through any of the four.
- **Impact**: `protectedRate`, `requireReviewRate`, `avgMergeRate`, `avgAiGovernedRate`, and the fleet activity sparkline are security/governance-posture numbers shown on the org security view and digest. A wrong denominator (counting unreadable/empty repos) understates or overstates fleet protection — a compliance-grade misstatement.
- **Fix sketch**: Extract pure folders — `foldPrStats(rawList)`, `foldGovernance(rawList)`, `sumActivitySeries(seriesList)`, `groupDiscrepancies(rawList)` — each taking already-fetched raw strings/objects. Assert: malformed JSON and schema-mismatched rows are excluded *without* corrupting the rate (denominator = valid rows only); `sumActivitySeries` right-aligns correctly for mixed-length inputs (a 2-week and a 4-week repo sum at the most-recent week, not week 0); an all-invalid input returns the documented `null`/empty shape, not a throw.

## 4. Test getOrgBacklog's projectedPoints ROI assembly and overdue/dueSoon counting
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Category**: edge-case
- **File**: src/lib/db/org-insights.ts:372-465 (per-repo ROI + counts + grouping)
- **Scenario**: The backlog computes engine-true `projectedPoints` per item via `projectedGain(dims, archetype, dimId)` but only when `dims.length > 0 && scan` — scans predating persisted dimensions must project `null`, not `0`. It also counts `overdue` (dueInDays `< 0`) and `dueSoon` (`>= 0 && <= 7`) and groups by owner with "Unassigned last". `dueBucketFor` is tested in isolation, but the *integration* — that a null-dims scan yields `projectedPoints: null` (not a misleading `+0 pts`), that `gain.points === 0` is preserved as `null` via the `gain ? gain.points : null` ternary (note: a legitimate 0-point gain is currently coerced to null), and that `overdue`/`dueSoon`/`assigned` counts stay consistent with the grouped lists — is unverified.
- **Root cause**: The assembly loop is inline in the async function; the `gain ? gain.points : null` truthiness check conflates "no dims" with "zero projected gain", an untested semantic that misreports ROI for an already-maxed dimension.
- **Impact**: Leaders prioritize the backlog on "+N pts · unlocks LX". A scan with no persisted dims silently showing `+0` (or a real 0-gain item showing `null`) mis-sorts the roadmap; an `overdue` count that drifts from the by-due list erodes trust in the planning surface.
- **Fix sketch**: Extract `buildBacklogItems(repos, now): { items, counts }` (pure over already-fetched rows). Assert: a scan with empty `dimensions` → `projectedPoints: null`; a genuine 0-point `projectedGain` is represented distinctly from "no data" (decide and lock the semantic); `overdue` + `dueSoon` totals equal the sizes of the corresponding `byDue` buckets; Unassigned owner group sorts last regardless of overdue count.

## 5. Pin the org-level recommendation leverage formula against silent re-weighting
- **Severity**: Low
- **Category**: coverage-gap
- **File**: src/lib/db/org-insights.ts:185-198 (getOrgRecommendations leverage); src/lib/db/org-shared.ts:5-6 (LEVEL_RANK / IMPACT_WEIGHT)
- **Scenario**: `leverage = round(repoCount · IMPACT_WEIGHT[impact] · (1 + dimWeight) · 10) / 10`, with dedup keyed on `dimId::title` and "strongest impact wins" across repos. The sort is `leverage desc, then repoCount desc`. None of this — the impact-max merge, the dim-weight multiplier, the rounding, or the tie-break — is tested. A change to `IMPACT_WEIGHT`, `weightsFor("org")`, or the rounding would silently reorder the org's "highest-leverage moves" list with no failing test.
- **Root cause**: Pure map/reduce buried in an async DB function; an LLM-generatable test batch over a small fixed input closes it in minutes against a real invariant.
- **Impact**: This list is the dashboard's "do these first" recommendation; a silent re-ranking misdirects effort, but blast radius is bounded (advisory, not money/security/data).
- **Fix sketch**: Extract `rankOrgRecommendations(perRepoRecs, weights): OrgRec[]`. Assert: two repos with the same `dimId::title` collapse to one group with `repoCount: 2` and the *higher* impact retained; leverage is monotonic in `repoCount` and in `IMPACT_WEIGHT`; ties on leverage break by `repoCount`; rounding gives one decimal place. Lock current `LEVEL_RANK`/`IMPACT_WEIGHT` values with a snapshot so an accidental edit fails loudly.
