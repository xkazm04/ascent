## Panel verdict — is repeated org-scanning worth the price?

**The single shared sentiment:** *the recurring READ is real and unusually honest — the trajectory GPS refuses to lie about a trend it cannot support — but the recurring PURCHASE is undecidable and, for half the panel, mis-shaped.* Almost nobody churns on the quality of the read; they churn (or downgrade, or stall) because **they cannot see the price at the moment of decision, the value the read promises is not co-located with the proof that it is real, or the read is measuring the wrong stack with false precision.** The product earns trust on the axis it was most likely to lose it (statistical over-claim) and loses the deal on the axes a maturity vendor usually takes for granted (price legibility, stack-fit, the surface that turns the read into a defended line item).

Decisively: **repetition is worth the price for a moving, web/JVM-shaped fleet on Enterprise (or a cheap honest-watch on Pro). It is NOT worth the price — as priced and surfaced today — for the mid-market priced buyer who cannot see the number, the non-web stack that is stably mismeasured, the burst/air-gap/OSS buyer the subscription shape does not fit, or the renewal-cutter who cannot prove a human opened it.**

### Segmented by company size

- **Solo (Priyanka, 1 dev) — should NOT pay.** At N=1 the recurring read rarely beats her own memory of a repo she wrote; the value lives on `/trends` (per-repo), not the team-shaped `/org` fleet she would never use; and the only tier that might help (Pro) shows no price. *Stay Free.* The honest trajectory is a genuine delight but saves ~10 min/cycle — below the bar.
- **SMB / bootstrapped (Yusuf 7, Klaus 25 eng) — pay only at a smaller, priced tier.** A 1-repo monolith makes the entire fleet vocabulary ("movers", "N repos leveled up") degenerate; the dedup-refund makes a slow repo a cheap honest regression watch (Klaus renews for exactly that) — but both grind teeth at an invisible Pro price and fleet machinery sized for a company they are not.
- **Scaleup / seed (Lena 15, Sofia 90, Tania 150, Arjun 50 eng) — the danger zone; mostly downgrade or churn.** They are on Team paying for headroom and tier-perks (365-day history) that are not wired, using <10% of the credit allotment, and hitting a stack-fit or value-realization wall (Sofia mobile-D3, Arjun ML, Tania engagement). This is where the most downgrades and the only FAIL live.
- **Mid-market (Gabriel 120, Mariam 80, Owen 60, Victor 300, Robert 2000 eng) — pay, but the manner of the upsell is the risk.** The read is worth it; the friction is forced-upgrade with no cost model (Gabriel hits the 402, then "contact us"), a phantom upgrade axis (Mariam retention), a privacy engine two tiers up (Owen), or an un-right-sizeable tier (Victor). They renew/upgrade *despite* the product, not because of it.
- **Enterprise (Anika 400, Theo 15-cos, Diane gov) — price is not the lever; the missing recurring SURFACE is.** They already signed; what is at stake is whether the read does the job — a fleet adoption curve (Anika), a fleet-of-fleets (Theo), or running in the boundary at all (Diane). Renew if the surface ships; the trajectory honesty already clears their bar.
- **Mega (Sasha 10k) — build-vs-buy: buy the smallest tier for the one thing she cannot build (the corpus), reproduce the rest.** The scoring math is a squad-quarter; only the cross-org corpus + honest forecast are non-reproducible, and they render at Pro — so she downgrades, not churns.
