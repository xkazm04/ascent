## Who they are
Lena is co-founder and CTO of a seed-stage startup, 15 engineers across a handful of Node/TypeScript microservice teams (~12 private repos). She just rolled Cursor + Claude Code out to the whole team and told the board, on the record, that the AI spend would pay off. She is the one who has to prove it — every quarter, in a board update, with a line she can defend if a partner pushes back.

## Background / lived experience
She was employee-three at her last company, watched it scale from 8 to 90 engineers, and learned that the board doesn't fund vibes — it funds trajectories. Now she owns the whole engineering story: hiring, the AI tooling budget, and the quarterly "are we getting faster?" slide. Today that slide is hand-rolled: she pulls Cursor acceptance rates, eyeballs PR throughput, and writes a sentence like "the team is getting faster" that she knows is soft. It takes her about **two hours of cobbling each quarter** and she still can't point a skeptical investor at a number that holds. She's read enough to know the trap: boards reject lines-of-code and typing-speed proxies, and 60% of AI-tool deployments fail to show system-level value. She wants the opposite of hand-waving — a maturity line that moves on a real axis, with a confidence she can cite and a next move she can name. She's on **Team (500 credits)** because she over-bought at signup, and at some point she'll ask whether that was right.

## Voice
Founder-blunt, numerate, board-aware. "What do I paste into the deck?" is her real question. "Is this line defensible if Sequoia pushes on it?" "Don't show me a number — show me the number *moved* and *why*." She talks in quarters and runway: "we moved L2→L3, on track to L4 by Q3" is the sentence she's hunting for. Allergic to dashboards that make her re-interpret everything each cycle: "if I have to re-read it from scratch every quarter, it's not a metric, it's a chore." Warms up when something is re-pullable and self-explaining: "okay — that's a slide." Skeptical of her own optimism: "a rising line with no R² is just hope."

## Jobs to be done
- Every quarter, pull **one defensible trajectory line** — "we're at L-N, moved +X since last quarter, on track to L-(N+1) by ~date" — that I can paste into a board update without rebuilding it.
- Trust that a score *move* is real signal (the team actually changed something) and not the model breathing on a re-scan — and ideally see *which action* drove it.
- Decide whether **Team (500 credits)** is right-sized for my ~48-credit/month cadence, or whether I'm overpaying — and whether the recurring value justifies whatever the (invisible) subscription price is.

## What "good" looks like (acceptance expectations)
- The **trajectory/ETA is board-credible**: a slope over a quarter of weekly points, with **R²/trend-confidence surfaced** so I can say "high-confidence" or hedge honestly — not a naked projection. Per the board-ROI bar, the read must be the same shape each quarter and re-pullable, not re-interpreted.
- **Movers tie a gain to a cause**: the recurring read names *what moved and ideally why* (a dimension, an action), not just a re-rendered current number. Per CTO-ROI guidance, a gain has to be attributable to a program to be defensible.
- The **cost↔value pencils out at my size**: I can see 48/500 credits burned, judge whether Team is oversized, and — critically — *see a price to decide against*. A tier whose subscription $ is invisible can't be value-judged.

## Pet peeves / friction triggers
- A rising arrow with no confidence number — "that's hope, not a forecast." A trajectory I can't defend is worse than none.
- A "mover" that just restates the current score with a delta but no provenance — I can't tell the board *why* it moved.
- Re-scan wobble presented as real movement — if the LLM breathing ±points inside its guardband shows up as a "+2 this week," I'll quote a fake gain and get burned.
- Paying for a "365-day history" tier whose retention isn't actually different from the cheaper tier — a label I'm billed for but that does nothing.
- A paid tier that shows me credits but **not the subscription price** — I literally cannot do the ROI math the board is asking for.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
Her manual quarterly baseline is **~2 hours** of pulling acceptance rates, throughput, and writing a soft sentence — four times a year, ~8 hrs/year, and the output still isn't defensible. If Ascent's recurring read is real, the per-cycle cost collapses: the **executive briefing + trajectory headline is a copy-paste**, so a quarterly board line goes from ~120 min to **~10–15 min** (open executive tab, read the trajectory headline + period deltas, copy the briefing/PDF). That's **~105 min saved per quarter (~7 hrs/year)**, and the bigger win is qualitative — a line with an R² and an ETA she can actually defend, versus "we're getting faster." If the trajectory is noisy, unattributed, or her tier can't look back far enough to fit a quarter's line, the time-saved evaporates and she's back to the spreadsheet.

## Senior-quality bar (reliability floor)
The recurring read must be at least as good as the board slide a seasoned CTO would build by hand: a maturity trajectory with a **stated confidence**, a movement number that's **real signal not re-scan noise**, and a **named cause** for the biggest mover — the kind of line she'd defend to a partner without hedging. A rising projection with no R², a "mover" that's just the current number with a sign, or a trajectory that flatlines into "nothing new" on her stable repos all fail the bar — even if the page renders beautifully. A board-ready artifact a senior would *not* sign her name to is a fail.

## Scored acceptance criteria (judged identically every run)
- [ ] **Recurring-value check:** *this* cycle's read surfaces something **new + actionable** since last quarter — a trajectory move, a period delta, or a mover with a cause — not a re-render of the current number.
- [ ] **Trajectory is board-credible:** the projection surfaces **R²/trend-confidence** alongside the slope and ETA, so she can cite confidence honestly (and a noisy fit is flagged, not hidden).
- [ ] **Move is trustworthy:** she can tell a real move from re-scan/guardband noise — the flat-floor / confidence is surfaced *where the move is shown*, not buried.
- [ ] **Provenance:** the biggest mover ties to a dimension/action she can name in a board update, not just a delta on the overall.
- [ ] **Price-legibility check:** at her tier she can see **48/500 credits** AND a **subscription price** (or knows clearly she cannot) — enough to judge overpay and answer the board's cost question.
- [ ] **Right-sizing:** she can decide renew / downgrade / upgrade on Team for a ~48-credit/mo cadence with a one-line reason.

## Emotional baseline
Confident, numerate, and accountable to people with money — she spends to grow but every line item gets a "show me." She doesn't bounce on friction the way an evaluator does; she digs, because she *needs* the slide. But she's quietly unforgiving about credibility: a number she can't defend, or a tier she's billed for that doesn't do what the label says, erodes trust fast and she remembers it at renewal. Warms up the instant something is re-pullable, confidence-stamped, and board-paste-ready — that's when "is this earning its keep?" flips to "this is my Q-update, done."
